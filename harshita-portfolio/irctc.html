Introduction

IRCTC is India's primary railway booking platform, used by millions daily. The surface-level problem — a cluttered, outdated interface — masked the deeper issue: users couldn't trust what they were seeing. Is the booking confirmed? Did the payment go through? Is this the right train?

This redesign focused on rebuilding that trust through clarity, feedback, and a streamlined booking flow — making the experience faster, more transparent, and accessible across devices.

Design Framing

IRCTC users don't fail because the UI is ugly. They fail because the system doesn't tell them what's happening. The redesign starts there — not at aesthetics, but at feedback and confidence.

Problem

What was breaking — and why it mattered.

  • No confirmation feedback after payment — Users couldn't tell if their booking had succeeded or failed. No clear error. No retry. Just ambiguity.
  • Trust collapse at every decision point — Seat availability, fare class, train timing — all required clicking into sub-menus to verify.
  • 6+ steps before reaching payment — No progress indicator. Users dropped off mid-flow, unsure how far they were from completing.
  • Filters reset between searches — Date, class, quota preferences had to be re-entered every time.
  • Mobile experience broken — Navigation patterns designed for desktop. CTAs misaligned, tap targets too small.
  • No predictive search — Station entry required exact spelling. Partial matches returned errors.
Existing IRCTC interface — booking flow, payment, navigation, mobile
Solution

Rebuilding trust — not just redesigning screens.

The redesign addressed three failure zones: the booking flow (reduced from 6 steps to 3), the payment confirmation (added real-time feedback and error recovery), and the search and filter experience (persistent filters, predictive station search).

Redesigned flow — streamlined booking, payment feedback, persistent filters

01 · Heuristic Evaluation

Evaluating IRCTC against Nielsen's 10 heuristics

The existing platform was evaluated across four critical flows: search, booking, payment, and account management. Violations were rated by severity (1–4) and tagged by heuristic.

Visibility of system status
No feedback during critical transitions

After submitting payment, the system showed a loading state with no indication of success or failure.

Severity 4 — Catastrophic. Payment is the highest-stakes moment.
Match with real world
Railway terminology without explanation

Terms like "WL," "GNWL," "PQWL," and fare classes appeared without definitions.

Severity 3 — Major. Affects every non-frequent traveler.
User control and freedom
No way to edit booking mid-flow

Changing passenger details or seat class required starting the entire booking over.

Severity 3 — Major. Every correction restarts a 6-step process.
Error prevention + recovery
Payment errors with no recovery path

Failed payments showed a generic error. No retry option, no saved details, no guidance.

Severity 4 — Catastrophic. Primary dropout trigger.

02 · User Research

Contextual interviews and observational tasks

Conducted with users across experience levels — first-time IRCTC users, regular travelers, and users who had abandoned bookings mid-flow. The goal: understand what the experience felt like at the moment it broke.

Tasks observed: Search and select a train route, complete a booking to payment, recover from a simulated payment failure. Time, errors, and emotional response tracked at each stage.

03 · Define

Three failure zones emerged

Heuristic violations and user research findings were synthesized into a clustered affinity map. Violations grouped by the decision point in the booking flow where they occurred — not by heuristic category.

Three failure zones: the booking flow (too long, no progress signal), the payment moment (no feedback, no recovery), and the search experience (no persistence, no intelligence).

Design Challenge

How might we rebuild user confidence at every critical decision point in the IRCTC booking flow — so that completing a booking feels certain, not anxious?

DESIGN
From violation map to redesigned flows
Design
01 · Brainstorming

Three failure zones, three solution directions

Each failure zone was addressed separately. For booking — how to reduce steps without losing information? For payment — what does real-time feedback look like? For search — what does persistence and predictive behavior require?

Solutions evaluated against two criteria: does this address a heuristic violation, and does it reduce cognitive load at the moment users needed it most?

Design
02 · Low Fidelity & Mid Fidelity

From IA changes to structured wireframes

Lo-fi sketches mapped information architecture changes — what lives where, what gets removed, what gets promoted. Mid-fidelity wireframes in Figma structured the revised booking flow, payment feedback states, and persistent search filters.

Key mid-fi decisions: step progress indicator in booking header, real-time payment status states (processing → success/failed), filter bar persisting across result pages.

Develop
02 · Testing

Three participant groups. Three tasks. Confidence measured.

Usability testing with first-time IRCTC users, regular travelers, and previous booking abandoners. Tasks: complete a search, complete a booking through payment, recover from a simulated payment failure.

What we measured: Task completion rate, time to completion, number of errors, and confidence rating at the payment stage — specifically because it was the primary trust failure identified in the evaluation.

Impact
Booking felt faster. Payment felt certain. Trust held.

The redesigned flow reduced cognitive load at every critical decision point. Step progress eliminated ambiguity. Real-time payment feedback gave users the confidence to complete transactions.

Booking Flow
6 screens → 3 steps

Removed redundant confirmation screens. Progress indicator added throughout.

Payment Feedback
Silent failure → real-time states

Processing, success, and failure states with explicit messaging. Retry without restarting.

Search & Filters
Reset → persistent

Filters persist across result pages. Predictive station search reduces input errors.

Terminology
Jargon → plain language

WL, GNWL, fare codes given plain-language explanations inline.

Note: All outcomes are qualitative observations from usability testing. Quantitative metrics would require longitudinal measurement on the live platform.
